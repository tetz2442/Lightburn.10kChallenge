Launch

  • 7/16/1969 9:32:00 AM

    The Greatest Single Step in Human History - A trip to the Moon. Apollo 11 blasts off from launch Pad.

  • The launch comes after a 28-hour countdown. It takes place in highly suitable weather, with winds 10 knots from the southeast, temperature in the mid-80's, and clouds at 15,000 feet.

Travel

  • 07/16/69 9:43:39 AM

    Saturn Stages Fired one after another. Apollo 11 enters a 103 nautical mile - high earth orbit.

  • 07/16/69 9:45:21 AM

    Orbital Navigation Begins

  • 07/16/69 12:22:03 PM

    Third Stage engine firing boosts Apollo 11 out of orbit midway. In it's second trip around the Earth lunar trajectory is at an initial speed of 24,200 MPH.

  • 07/16/69 12:49:04 PM

    The Lunar land craft (Code name Eagle) is unpacked from its compartment atop the launch rockets. Explosive bolts are used to separate the adapter and blow apart four panels that make up its side, exposing The Eagle.

  • 07/16/69 08:48:00 PM

    Mission control bids crew goodnight - The spacecraft is about 22,000 nautical miles from earth traveling at a speed of 12,914 ft per second.

  • 07/18/69 04:40:00 PM

    TV Transmission Started - Viewers are shown the inside of command module and the view of Earth about 128,000 nautical miles away. Broadcast lasted 36 minutes.

  • 07/16/69 08:48:00 PM

    2nd TV Transmission - Spacecraft is now 175,000 nautical miles from Earth and 48,000 from the moon.

  • 07/19/69 10:31:00 PM

    Astronauts make remarks to Houston: "It's (the moon) about three-quarters of the hatch window and, of course, we can see the entire circumference, even though part of it is in complete shadow and part of it's in earth-shine. It's a view worth the price of the trip."

  • 07/19/69 01:13:00 PM

    The vessel passes completely behind the moon and out of radio contact with earth for the first time.

Moon

  • 07/19/69 04:17:39 PM

    Landing - Craft settles down with a jolt, and the words "The Eagle has landed" are immediatley radioed in to Mission control by Armstrong.

  • 07/19/69 10:39:33 PM

    Preparation of Lander: Later than the proposed time of 6:00pm- Armstrong opens the Lunar Module hatch and squeezes through the opening with his portable life support and communications weighing 84 lbs on earth (14lbs on Moon).

  • 07/19/69 10:56:15 PM

    1st Step - Armstrong puts his left foot to the moon. It is the first time in history that a person has ever stepped on anything that has not existed on or originated from earth.

    Famous words: "That's on small step for man, one giant leap for mankind".

  • 07/19/69 11:41:43 PM

    United States Flag Deployed - Two other U.S flags are brought and are now flown over the houses of Congress.

Travel

  • 07/21/69 01:54:00 PM

    Ascent engine is started and the Lunar module begins rising. Reaching vertical speed of 80 feet per second at 1,000 ft altitude.

  • 07/21/69 01:54:00 PM

    The Eagle re-docks with Columbia while circling on the back side of the moon.

Landing

  • 07/24/69 06:47:00 PM

    Crew awakens and begins to prepare for splashdown.

  • 07/24/69 12:32:00 PM

    Command Module re-enters Earth's atmosphere.

  • 07/24/69 12:51:00 PM

    Splash down 825 nautical miles southwest of Honolulu and about 13 nautical miles from recovery ship - the U.S.S. Hornet.

  • On June 1969 the Apollo astronauts flew to the moon with a guidance computer using 70k of memory - a testament of what we can do with a little when we put our minds to it.
